Offline First-Aid Guidelines

IF VICTIM IS UNRESPONSIVE AND NOT BREATHING, CALL 112 FIRST!
  1. Check responsiveness: Tap shoulders and shout "Are you okay?".
  2. Call 112: If no response, ask someone to call emergency and get an AED.
  3. Position the victim: Place flat on their back on a firm surface.
  4. Start Chest Compressions: Place heel of one hand on the center of chest, other hand on top. Push hard and fast (100-120 beats/min, 2 inches deep).
  5. Rescue Breaths: Give 2 breaths after every 30 compressions if trained. Otherwise, do Hands-Only CPR continuously.

  2. Apply direct pressure: Use a clean cloth or sterile bandage. Press firmly on the wound with your hands.
  3. Maintain pressure: Keep pressing for at least 5-10 minutes without looking.
  4. Elevate wound: Raise the injured limb above heart level to slow bleeding.
  5. Add layers: If blood seeps through, place another cloth on top. Do NOT remove the original dressing.
  6. Consider a Tourniquet: For severe limb bleeding that won't stop, apply a tourniquet 2-3 inches above the wound.
  1. Stop the burn: Remove the person from the heat or electrical source.
  2. Cool the burn: Run cool, gentle running tap water over the burn for 10-20 minutes. Do NOT use ice or freezing water.
  3. Remove clothing: Gently take off jewelry, belts, or clothing near the burn, unless stuck to the skin.
  4. Protect the burn: Cover loosely with a clean, dry, non-stick bandage or plastic wrap.
  5. Avoid home remedies: Do NOT apply butter, toothpaste, oils, or ointments as they trap heat and invite infection.

  2. Do NOT realign the bone: Never try to push a protruding bone back or reset it.
  3. Immobilize the limb: Apply a splint using rigid cardboard, rolled newspapers, or sticks, securing it above and below the fracture site.
  4. Apply cold pack: Wrap ice in a towel and apply to ease swelling (do not place ice directly on skin).
  5. Keep victim still: Prevent movement and keep the person warm until paramedics arrive to prevent shock.
